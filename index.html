For Nurse Leaders

Culture problems hide
in plain sight.

Most organizations rely on annual engagement surveys to understand nurse culture. But nurses know those surveys aren't truly anonymous. They know responses get back to managers. So they say what's safe, not what's true.

Fear of retaliation, lack of anonymity, and poorly designed questions suppress the truth leaders need to see. Without clear signals from the frontline, leaders are left guessing about what is really driving burnout, turnover, and disengagement.

How It Works

A protected signal window
for frontline truth.

01
Protected Entry
A confidential submission window opens for nurses on the unit. Using the UNFOLD™ framework, nurses share lived experience through seven structured reflection prompts, anonymously, on their own time, from any device.
02
Signal Distillation
All responses are synthesized using the Heartfold Insight™ culture intelligence framework, developed by a board-certified Clinical Nurse Specialist with 12+ years of frontline and system-level nursing experience.
03
Executive Brief
Leaders receive a clear, decision-ready culture intelligence report identifying hidden pressure points, psychological safety gaps, and evidence-based recommendations, without ever exposing individual voices.

Our Story

Built by a nurse.
For the ones still showing up.

I built HeartVAULT because I kept watching brilliant nurses leave, and the people in charge kept saying they didn't know why.

They had surveys. They had town halls. They had open door policies. What they didn't have was a space where nurses could actually tell the truth without it costing them something.

I'm a DNP-prepared Clinical Nurse Specialist with over a decade at the bedside and in systems leadership. I've watched what happens when nurses speak up. I've watched what happens when they stop. I've lived both sides of that story.

HeartVAULT is not a survey. It is a protected digital sanctuary where nurses can reflect honestly, and where leaders receive the kind of culture intelligence that actually tells them what to do about it.

Because the data has always existed. It was just never safe enough to say out loud.

Dr. Vanessa Abacan

DNP, APRN-CNS, ACCNS-P, RNC-MNN, EBP-C · Founder, Heartfold Healing℠
